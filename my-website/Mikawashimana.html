Mikawashima Greens

Mikawashima rape is one of the most popular pickles in Edo, and is even depicted in books of the time. There is a record that it was offered to the shogun who visited the city for falconry as a tasty pickles. Pickled in salt, it was prized as a valuable leafy vegetable in the wintertime. With the introduction of Chinese cabbage to Japan, it was no longer cultivated and became a “phantom vegetable” indeed. Recently, through the efforts of the Edo Tokyo Traditional Vegetable Study Group, farmers in the western part of Tokyo have begun to cultivate the “blue-stemmed Mikawashima rape,” a descendant of the Mikawashima rape, from the “Sendai basho rape,” and it is now being grown as an Edo Tokyo traditional vegetable. It has made a comeback.
