Shioiri Radish

In the past, the Arakawa River land was suitable for growing daikon radish, and various types of daikon were cultivated. As the names suggest, the Shioiri daikon grown in Shioiri (now Minami-Senju) in Hashiba Village and the Arakida daikon grown in Arakida (now Machiya) are both two-year old daikon (meaning “produced over two years”) that are sown in the fall and harvested in the spring after a year has passed. The sandy soil of Shioiri and Arakida was suitable for growing daikon and produced particularly good quality. It is a long, thin daikon with a white neck, and was widely eaten as grated radish, sashimi garnish, simmered dish, and pickles.
